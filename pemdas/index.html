Order of Operations

Parentheses
Parentheses are a pair of brackets used to mark a certain set of numbers. Completing mathematical processes within the parentheses is the first step.

    Parentheses

    Exponents
    Exponents are a numerical value that represents the power to which a number or expression is being raised. It is typically expressed as a raised, smaller number.

      Exponents

      Multiplication
      Multiplication is a mathematical process that repeats the addition of a given number. Completing multiplication expressions is the third step of the order of operations.

        Multiplication

        Division
        Like multiplication, division is a mathematical process that splits a number into equal parts or groups. It comes after multiplication and is the fourth step of the process.

          Division

          Addition
          Addition is a basic arithmetic process that finds the total amount of two or more combined values. It is denoted by the + symbol and is one of the final steps.

            Addition

            Subtraction
            Like Addition, subtraction is a basic mathematical process that tells us the difference between a set of values. It is the opposite of addition and the final step to the process!
