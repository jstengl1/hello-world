Exponents

Exponents are the second step to the order of operations. To do this, locate the exponents and multiply the origin number by the amount indicated in the exponent place. If an exponent is placed within parentheses, it is the first step completed after isolating the parentheses. Exponents are indicated by a caret symbol ^ or a superscript number. Before doing multiplication, division, addition, and subtraction processes, exponents must be completed!

Example!
5 X 2^2
5 X 4
= 20
